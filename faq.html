Frequently Asked Questions

Before you step on board.

Here are the most common questions about gear, guest capacity, food, catches, weather, launch point, and trip preparation.

Common Questions

Do I need to bring my own fishing rods and reels?

No. Your charter includes access to premium rod and reel setups, specialized jigs, and trolling lures prepared for the deep channels around Danajon Bank. Guests may still bring a personal rod if preferred.

Can we bring more than 4 people if some are only watching?

No. The boat is strictly capped at a maximum of 4 guests total, whether they are fishing or spectating. This maintains safety, deck space, comfort, and a premium private experience.

Are food and drinks provided?

Food and drinks are not provided. Guests must bring their own meals, snacks, and beverages. Ice and cooler support are provided on board.

Can we keep the fish we catch?

Yes. The catch belongs to the guests. The crew will help bleed, wash, ice, and store the fish to preserve quality before returning to base.

Are photos and videos included?

Yes. The charter includes media documentation using on-board camera equipment. Trip footage and photos may be transferred after the session.

What happens if the weather is bad?

If the captain determines that sea or weather conditions are unsafe, guests may choose a priority reschedule date or a 100% refund.

Where is the launch point?

The base of operations is Hadsan Beach Park, Lapu-Lapu City, Mactan, Cebu, Philippines.

Are accommodations available near the launch point?

Hadsan Beach Park has on-site accommodations and nearby dining options, which may be convenient for early morning departures or late returns from nocturnal sessions.

When should we arrive?

Guests should arrive 15 minutes before the scheduled departure time at the Hadsan Beach Park meeting point.

Are alcohol and smoking allowed?

Moderate alcohol consumption may be allowed, but unsafe, rowdy, or impaired behavior can result in the captain terminating the charter. Smoking is restricted for fire safety reasons.
