Common Questions

What most guests ask first.

These are the most common questions guests ask before booking a private offshore fishing charter.

01

Are there any accommodations available at your launch site?

Yes. Hadsan Beach Park offers comfortable overnight rooms right on-site. This is ideal if you want to stay near the dock the night before an early 5:00 AM Morning Session, or relax immediately after pulling into port from a 12-hour night session. The beach park also has an on-site coffee shop and multiple restaurants, making it a convenient base camp for your group.

02

Do I need to bring my own fishing rod and reels?

No. Your charter rate includes premium rod and reel rentals, along with specialized trolling lures and jigs optimized for the deep channels around Danajon Bank. You will fish using elite international gear including the electric Shimano Beast Master 6000, the two-speed Shimano Talica 50II, heavy-duty Daiwa Saltiga 35N/LD35, and the high-drag Bullzen Anunaki 50N. If you have a favorite lucky rod, you are welcome to bring it on board.

03

Can we bring more than 4 people if some just want to watch or take photos?

No. The New Zealand-built Extreme 5.7M Game King is strictly capped at a maximum of 4 guests total. This applies regardless of whether guests are actively fishing or only spectating. This safety cap preserves deck space, comfortable movement inside the enclosed hardtop cabin, and a premium, uncrowded experience for everyone on board.

04

Are drinks and meals provided on the boat?

Food and drinks are not provided. Anglers must bring their own meals, snacks, and beverages for the 6-hour, 12-hour, or full-day session. A large commercial-grade marine cooler packed with fresh ice is provided to keep your food and drinks cold throughout the trip.

05

Do you charge extra to give us the fishing videos and photos?

No. Media documentation is complimentary and included in both Tier 1 and Tier 2 packages. The crew uses an onboard dual-camera setup consisting of a GoPro Hero 13 for ultra-HD deck view footage and a DJI Osmo Action 4 for low-light night sessions. Raw footage and hero photos can be transferred directly to your smartphone through Quick-Pair WiFi or AirDrop before you leave.

06

What happens to the fish we catch? Can we keep them?

Yes. The catch belongs entirely to you. The crew will immediately bleed, wash down, and ice-store your fish in the onboard coolers to maintain fresh, restaurant-grade quality. Upon returning to Hadsan Beach Park, the deckhand can assist with bagging your catch.

07

Are alcohol and smoking allowed on board?

Moderate consumption of canned beer or cocktails brought by guests is allowed. However, rowdy, impaired, or unsafe behavior can result in the captain immediately terminating the charter and returning to Hadsan Beach Park with no refund. For fire safety around the Yamaha F150FETX fuel lines, smoking is restricted to designated areas of the deck away from the motor console.

08

What happens if a client accidentally drops a rod or reel overboard?

Normal wear, jig losses, and line breaks are covered by the charter. However, guests are financially responsible for the direct replacement cost of any rod or reel dropped completely overboard due to negligence or horseplay. Safety lanyards are available upon request to help eliminate this risk.

09

What happens if the weather is bad?

Guest safety is the absolute priority. If the captain determines that sea conditions or weather forecasts in the Camotes Sea or Middle Pass are unsafe for the aluminum hull, the team will notify you immediately. You may reschedule to the next available date or receive an immediate, unconditional 100% refund of your deposit.

Launch Point

Hadsan Beach Park, Lapu-Lapu City

The charter operates from Mactan’s water edge, helping guests avoid unnecessary mainland travel before the offshore run.
