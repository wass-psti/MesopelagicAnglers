About the Project

A tribute to the strange intelligence of the deep sea.

The mesopelagic zone sits roughly between 200 and 1,000 meters below the ocean surface. It is too deep for strong sunlight, yet not fully dark. Many animals here survive through camouflage, pressure adaptation, vertical migration, and bioluminescence.

Bioluminescence

Many deep-sea creatures generate light to lure prey, communicate, hide, or confuse predators.

Adaptation

Life in this layer must survive cold temperatures, low food supply, and immense pressure.

Mystery

The deep sea remains one of Earth’s least explored environments. Basically Earth’s final DLC.

The Mesopelagic Zone

The ocean’s twilight layer.

This zone is a biological transition area. Sunlight fades, photosynthesis becomes impossible, and animals rely on stealth, light organs, specialized eyes, and energy-efficient movement.

Epipelagic Zone

0–200 m · Sunlit surface waters

Mesopelagic Zone

200–1,000 m · Twilight zone

Bathypelagic Zone

1,000–4,000 m · Midnight zone

Abyssopelagic Zone

4,000–6,000 m · Abyssal zone
